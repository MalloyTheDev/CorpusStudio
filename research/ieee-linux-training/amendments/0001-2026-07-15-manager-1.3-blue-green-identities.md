## Reason

The base matrix named prospective first-party environment IDs ending in v1. Those placeholders were
never sealed as the matched research pair. Subsequent bring-up exposed two evidence changes that
make every manager-1.2 environment inadmissible for a new run:

1. The first-party worker now requires stricter successful-training evidence, including canonical
   adapter update and export-state identity, materialized-gradient coverage, a real optimizer, one
   finite loss per exact optimizer step, and artifact-bound measured-fit admission.
2. Environment Manager 1.3 requires complete all-row RECORD evidence for every installed package:
   record_verified_entries, record_entries, and installed_file_count must be positive and equal.

A new worker wheel and new immutable environment locks are therefore required. Reusing, recreating,
renaming, or relabeling a prior environment would break the identity chain and the study's evidence
boundary.

## Affected cells and execution stages

The environment identity substitution affects:

- both first-party 0.5B bring-up smoke paths;
- all 30 not-yet-executed primary cells (3 models x 5 sequence lengths x 1 adapter method x 2
  first-party paths);
- all 16 not-yet-executed first-party secondary adapter cells (2 models x 2 sequence lengths x 2
  adapter methods x 2 first-party paths); and
- any later adaptive follow-up that selects a first-party path.

The Unsloth execution path is not changed by this amendment. No primary or secondary cell had been
executed when this amendment was authored. The two new environments must be built from the same
wheel bytes and matched package artifacts, while retaining their separately sealed math and flash
capability tuples.

## Evidence visible before this change

The following evidence was visible and is disclosed to avoid outcome-dependent ambiguity:

- The manager-1.2 v2 math and flash environments each passed their separate tiny environment probe.
- One sequence-256, three-step bring-up attempt was dispatched once per v2 path. Both reached real
  model placement and adapter insertion, then failed before optimizer step 1 because an incoming
  BF16 autograd-edge tensor was incorrectly treated as a materialized FP32 leaf gradient. Neither
  attempt produced a successful artifact or a paper-matrix result.
- The manager-1.2 v3 pair and fresh sequence-256 RunPlans were created but never dispatched. Their
  locks used the older partial RECORD-count meaning, so Environment Manager 1.3 must reject them for
  new admission.
- No real optimizer step had passed through platform-run, and sequence length 4096 remained
  unverified.

These records remain historical evidence under their original IDs and hashes. They are not inputs to
new admission and are not reclassified as preregistered paper results.

## Prospective status and compatibility

The identity substitutions and stronger admission records are prospective. Protocol 1.0.0 and its
base matrix remain available unchanged. Protocol 1.1.0 uses the complete versioned effective matrix;
it does not reinterpret a 1.0.0 result.

RESERVED_IDENTITIES.v1.json binds the preserved evidence manifests and enumerates existing
environment, lock, worker, plan, execution, run, output, artifact, and evidence-root identity
classes. New evidence must be set-disjoint for every enumerated class. A v4 lock or worker change
requires a fresh RunPlan and run ID. No v1, v2, or v3 environment lock, plan ID, plan hash, execution
hash, run ID, output path, or artifact identity may be copied into v4 evidence.

Primary analyses may include only trials that record protocol version 1.1.0 and the exact effective
matrix identity. This amendment is not retrospective and does not authorize a GPU workload by
itself.
